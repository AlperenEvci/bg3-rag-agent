                Dragonborn

                Racial Features

                Dragonborn is a Race that will be added in Baldur's Gate 3. Dragonborns primary ability is Strength. Note, the info on this page is based on data mining and DND player books and is subject to change.

                • BG3 Full Game Note: In the full launch of Baldur's Gate 3, Ability Score Points for Races that were present in Early Access are removed. And instead, Ability Score Points in the full game correspond with the Class players will choose.

                 

                A proud race that values clan and skills above all else. Once enslaved by dragons, they strive to be self-sufficient, not wanting to be beholden to anyone, not even the gods.

                 

                Dragonborn Traits:

                • Draconic Ancestry. You are distantly related to a particular kind of dragon. Choosing your dragon ancestor determines the damage and area of your breath weapon, and the type of resistance you gain:
                • Black Dragonborn: Acid (DEX save)
                • Blue Dragonborn: Lightning (DEX save)
                • Brass Dragonborn: Fire (DEX save)
                • Bronze Dragonborn: Lightning (DEX save)
                • Copper Dragonborn: Acid (DEX save)
                • Gold Dragonborn: Fire (DEX save)
                • Green Dragonborn: Poison (CON save)
                • Red Dragonborn: Fire (DEX save)
                • Silver Dragonborn: Cold (CON save)
                • White Dragonborn: Cold (CON save)
                • Breath Weapon: You can use your action to exhale destructive energy. It deals damage in an area according to your ancestry. When you use your breath weapon, all creatures in the area must make a saving throw, the type of which is determined by your ancestry. The DC of this saving throw is 8 + your Constitution modifier + your proficiency bonus. A creature takes 2d6 damage on a failed save, and half as much damage on a successful one. Damage increases to 3d6 at level 6 and 4d6 at level 11. After using your breath weapon, you cannot use it again until you complete a long rest.

                Dragonborn Tips & Notes

                • In other sources, the Dragonborn race features +2 Strength and +1 Charisma. This will be changed on the full launch of BG3 and the Ability Score points are now associated with the Class that players will choose.
                • Most dragonborn have a conal breath, the only exception is Brass subrace which has a column shaped breath.
                • If romancing Lae'zel, dragonborn will gain higher approval with red dragonborn gaining the most.

                                                      For some reason the BG3 version of Dragonborn is even weaker than the 5e PHB version that most of the content in BG3 is based on. In PHB, breath weapon gains extra dice as you level (2d6 at level 1, 3d6 at level 5, 4d6 at level 11, 5d6 at level 16) - the same as Cantrip scaling. Additionally, the PHB version recharges on short or long rest.

                                                      Instead, the BG3 version does not increase from 2d6, and only recharges on a long rest. Without any other features given in compensation, BG3 Dragonborn is weaker than the PHB version - and they're generally not considered all that great even with the full PHB features.

                                                      In terms of the benefits of being a dragonborn in BG3, it's really only the damage resistance feature, and some dialog choices. With 2d6 maximum, small range/radius, and Long Rest only recharge, the breath weapon rapidly becomes weaker than nearly any other source of damage. Throwing items are directly comparable to this weapon even before feats, class features, or items that improve throwing items. In fact, thrown weapons are notably superior as they have a larger effective distance, comparable damage, no recharge, and can be part of an extra-attack cycle instead of consuming your entire action.

                                                      I really want to enjoy Dragonborn, but their racial features are unequivocally worse than all other races in BG3.
