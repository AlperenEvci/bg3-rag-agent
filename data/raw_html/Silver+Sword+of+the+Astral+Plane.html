                Silver Sword of the Astral Plane

                Legendary

                6~21 Damage

                2d6 +3  Slashing
                +1d6 Psychic (Conditional)

                Githborn Psionic Weapon: When wielded by a githyanki this weapon deals an additional 1~6 Psychic damage.

                Githborn Psionic Resistance: A githyanki holding this weapon has Advantage on Intelligence, Wisdom, and Charisma Saving Throws, Resistance to Psychic Damage, and cannot be Charmed.

                Weapon Enchantment +3

                Proficiency with this weapon type unlocks:
                  

                The fervour with which the githyanki express devotion to their queen is admirable to some, disquieting to others. When confronted with the fallibility of the divine (since no one is wholly perfect), who knows what reaction such folk might evince.

                Greatsword Two-Handed
                  2.7  1,300 

                Silver Sword of the Astral Plane is one of the Legendary Weapons in Baldur's Gate 3. It is a Legendary Greatsword. In BG3, each type of weapon has different ranges, damages, and other features (Finesse, Versatile, Dippable, etc.).  Characters need to master certain Proficiency before using a weapon, and sometimes gain special Actions while holding it.

                 The fervour with which the githyanki express devotion to their queen is admirable to some, disquieting to others. When confronted with the fallibility of the divine (since no one is wholly perfect), who knows what reaction such folk might evince.

                Information

                • Soulbreaker: Rend the enemy's body and soul, and possibly Stun them.

                • Githborne Psionic Weapon: When wielded by a githyanki this weapon deals an additional 1~6 Psychic Damage.

                • Githborn Psionic Resistance: A githyanki holding this weapon has Advantage on Intelligence, Wisdom, and Charisma Saving Throws, Reistance to Psychic Damage, and cannot be Charmed.

                • Range:  1.5m
                • Rarity: Legendary
                • Value: 1300
                • Weight: 2.7

                Location & Where to Find

                • Location: Sharess' Caress, from Kith'rak Voss 
                • Quest: The Githyanki Warrior (Take Raphael's deal and talk to Voss)
                • Dropped by:  Kith'rak Voss when using "Command: Drop" or Disarming Attack on him (ACT 1 ,  low chance)

                Notes & Tips for BG3

                • Disguise Self: Githyanki can be used by a non-gith to leverage Githborne Psionic Weapon psychic damage.
                • UUID: f01c3f5d-c542-420f-86c5-bdddf7819e29

                              Easiest way to get this in Act 1:

                              -Be Lae'zel
                              -Level 3+ Battlemaster fighter (choose disarming strike as one of your maneuvers)
                              -Drink hill giant potion
                              -Drink Potion of Invisibility
                              -Coat melee weapon in Oil of Accuracy
                              -Click turn based mode
                              -Walk up to Voss and use Disarming Attack (Melee)
                              -(92% Chance to hit)

                              Voss has +8 to his strength saving throws, so the strength check that he needs to fail (DC 15) has a 30% chance to drop the weapon. Save scum a bit if you have to, but this will make it take WAY less long than trying to use command (while he's holding the greatsword, he has advantage on wisdom saving throws which is why command is useless and less efficient)

                                                          - Position a character that is able to cast "Command: Drop" on the road behind the dragon (sneak/invis there).

                                                          - Save.
                                                          - Now cast "Command: Drop" on Voss, and see what happens.                                                           If they attack, or dialogue doesn't start after the cast, load and try again until you find the right spot.
                                                          - When in the right spot, save/load until he drops the sword.
                                                          - Now use summons to lure the hostile gith away from the sword. Make sure your party is very far away, so they won't get involved in the fight.
                                                          - Sneak next to sword, if necessary use throw to get it out of their sight, and pick it up.

                                                          This way, you can come back later to fight the gith for xp and loot. In my playthrough, they were far apart from each other after I had lured them away, which made the fight considerably easier.

                                                                                      if you are trying to steal this sword in act 1, here is the best method:
                                                                                      this works from level 3.
                                                                                      hire the fighter hireling (if you are level 4 take the feat for +2 in any stat and take strength)
                                                                                      bring your party up to the gate before the patrol, ungroup the fighter and have shadowheart cast guidance, and gale or wyll cast invisibility on the hireling.
                                                                                      go to camp with your 3 man party so they are not part of the fight, swap to hireling and run in behind voss, and activate turn based mode (quick save here after TBM for fast save scum)
                                                                                      use disarming strike from behind him, it has a 75% chance to hit with these stats but there is also the check for the disarm. look for 'weapon dropped' before the cutscene starts (it will be orange) if it doesn't drop you can immediately reload.
                                                                                      in the cutscene select the fighter dialogue option and then say nothing, this is the fastest way to combat with no checks.
                                                                                      when combat starts you can immediately pick up the sword and transfer it to another character in your party inventory.
                                                                                      hireling will die but you can just revive her and then pickpocket withers.

                                                                                      also regarding the racial bonuses: anyone who can cast shapeshift can access these additional buffs, just make sure you are githyanki when you equip the sword, then you can remove the shapeshift and keep the buff. great if you got the helmet from the deluxe edition as you can quickly make this full effectiveness for anyone.
