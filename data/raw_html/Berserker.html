                Berserker

                Initial Berserker Features
                • Frenzy
                • Frenzied Strike
                • Enraged Throw

                Inherited Barbarian Features
                • Strength Saving Throw Proficiency
                • Constitution Saving Throw Proficiency
                • Light Armour Proficiency
                • Medium Armour Proficiency
                • Shield Proficiency
                • Simple Weapon Proficiency
                • Martial Weapon Proficiency
                • Unarmoured Defense
                • Rage
                • Choose 2 Skill Proficiencies

                Berserker is a Subclass of Barbarian in Baldur's Gate 3. Barbarians primary ability is Strength, their saving throw proficiencies are Strength and Constitution, and they have a Hit Dice of 1d12. They have proficiency with Simple Weapons, martial weapons, light Armor, medium armor and shields.

                The Berserker subclass is a path within the Barbarian class in Dungeons & Dragons and Baldur's Gate 3. It emphasizes unrestrained fury and heightened offensive capabilities, transforming the Barbarian into a relentless and devastating force on the battlefield. 

                The defining feature of the Berserker subclass is the ability to enter a Frenzy while raging. When choosing to do so as a bonus action, the Barbarian gains an additional attack during each of their turns while they are in a rage. This intensifies their offensive power, allowing them to deal more damage and overwhelm opponents with a flurry of strikes.

                The Berserker subclass embodies the essence of unbridled rage and relentless offense.

                 

                Violence is both a means and an end. You follow a path of untrammeled fury, slick with blood, as you thrill in the chaos of battle, heedless of your own well-being.

                BG3 Berserker Features Progression

                • Level 3 - Frenzy (Action)
                • Level 3 - Frenzied Strike,  Enraged Throw (Bonus Action)
                • Level 6 - Mindless Rage (Subclass Feature)
                • Level 10 - Intimidating Presence (Action)

                 

                Berserker Tips & Builds

                • The Berserker subclass for the Barbarian characterizes for being a violent-blindly path of fury and rage.
                • At level 3, the Berserker unlocks Frenzy. Once activated, the Berserker can perform two new actions. Frenzied Strike and Enraged Throw. The former is a bonus action that allows the Berserker to attack with their melee weapon, but it also earns them a stack of Strain, which causes the Barbarian to suffer -1 to hit on all attacks per stack. The latter allows Berserkers to pick up an item or creature and throw it at a target, dealing Bludgeoning damage and knocking it Prone. Note that the strength of the Barbarian determines the amount of weight that can be thrown. Heavier items or characters, deal more damage.
                • At level 6, the Berserker learns Mindless Rage which makes the Berserker's frenzy immune to outside influence. This renders the berserker immune to Charmed or Frightened, while raging. Neither Calm Emotions can't end your rage. 
                • At level 10, the Berserker learns Intimidating Presence an action that can instill fear on a target, by menacing them. It is not a great ability and many barbarian players, decide to multiclass at level 9 to earn other Classes features and abilities.

                    So, I played this subclass during two playthroughs. Once as a pure melee great weapon martial and once as a throwing build (single class each time). Here is what I found:

                    The pure melee great weapon build can be great in the early and mid game. Fights tend to be somewhat in walking distance and a buffed barbarian can rage turn 1 and still reach one enemy. Reckless Attack works great with Great Weapon Master and there is basically no other ability that gives you unconditional access to advantage as early as Reckless Attack. This means, you can use Frenzied Strike turn 2 in optimal cases. This build tends to fall off drastically in the late game. Fighting arenas become much larger in Act 3 and reaching the enemy turn 1 becomes nearly impossible in many fights. Often, I had to use the Bonus Action on turn 1 on Jump or Misty Step and often did not even Rage on subsequent turns, since the Great Wepon Master Bonus Attack was often the better choice. At that point, just taking a Battle Master Fighter, jumping towards the enemy and using your Improved Extra Attack might be the better alternative. You can additionally multiclass Fighter into War Domain Cleric, to get a bonus weapon attack (Cleric 1/Fighter 11).

                    The throwing build is much better though. The combination of Tavern Brawler, the Ring of Throwing and the additional damage from Frenzy is crazy in the early game. You stay relevant throughout the game and you can support both an aggressive and an defensive party. You don't have the issue of reaching the enemy and can make full use of your attacks starting turn 1. You also do not collect Frenzied Strain, if using the bonus throw. You can multiclass into Eldritch Knight, to have thrown weapons return to you. This is not necessary, since there are two weapons that do so automatically and the later of the two is completely busted anyhow. If you want to play the thrower build, I recommend you read some guides and the wiki, because the returning weapons, especially the later one, are hard to find.

                    TLDR: If you want to play a Berserker, make it a throwing build. This might just be the best Barbarian build
