                College of Lore

                College of Lore Initial Features
                • Cutting Words
                • Bonus Proficiencies

                Inherited Bard Features
                • Dexterity Saving Throw Proficiency
                • Charisma Saving Throw Proficiency
                • Light Armour Proficiency
                • Simple Weapon Proficiency
                • Hand Crossbow Proficiency
                • Longsword Proficiency
                • Rapier Proficiency
                • Shortsword Proficiency
                • Musical Instrument Proficiency
                • Any 3 Skill Proficiencies
                • Spellcasting
                • Bardic Inspiration

                College of Lore is a Subclass of the Bard class in Baldur's Gate 3. Bards primary ability is Charisma, their saving throw proficiencies are Dexterity & Charisma, and they have a Hit Dice of 1d8. They have proficiency with Simple weapons, hand crossbows, longswords, rapiers, shortswords and with Light armor.

                You pursue beauty and truth, collecting knowledge from scholarly tomes to peasants' tales, and use your gifts to hold both audiences and enemies spellbound.

                 
                 
                 
                 

                 

                BG3 Bard Features Progression

                • Level 1 - Bardic Inspiration (Bonus Action)
                • Level 2 - Song of Rest (Action)
                • Level 2 - Jack of All Trades (Class Feature)
                • Level 3 - Expertise in 2 Skills (Class Feature)
                • Level 3 - Bard College (College of Lore, College of Valour, College of Swords)
                • Level 4 - Feat - Choose one from the Feats list
                • Level 5 - Font of Inspiration (Class Feature)
                • Level 5 - Improved Bardic Inspiration (Class Feature)
                • Level 5 - Bardic Inspiration Charge increases by 1
                • Level 6 - Countercharm (Action)
                • Level 8 - Feat - Choose one from the Feats list
                • Level 8 - Bardic Inspiration Charge increases by 1
                • Level 10 - Improved Bardic Inspiration (Class Feature)
                • Level 10 - Expertise in 2 Skills (Class Feature)
                • Level 10 - Choose 2 Spells from Magical Secrets spell list
                • Level 12 - Feat - Choose one from the Feats list

                BG3 College of Lore Features Progression

                • Level 3 - Cutting Words (Subclass Feature)
                • Level 3 - Bonus Proficiencies: Choose 3 Proficiencies of your choice (Subclass Feature)
                • Level 6 - Additional Magical Secrets: Choose 2 Spells from Magical Secrets spell list of Level 3 or lower

                COLLEGE OF LORE TIPS & BUILDS

                • College of Lore is a Bard subclass ideal for more spellcasting versatility, that can be used as a powerful support role. Try to stay away from the front lines, and provide support by debuffing enemies or buffing your party members.
                • College of Lore Bards learn Cutting Words which receive a 1d6 penalty to Attack Rolls, Ability Checks, and damage dealt until the start of your next turn. At level 5 and at level 10 the penalty is increased, to 1d8 and 1d10, respectively. Cutting Words is a great ability to debuff enemies.
                • At level 6, College of Lore Bards choose 2 Spells from Magical Secrets spell list of Level 3 or lower.
                • NEW! - New Spells have been added to the Bard's Magical Secrets Spells List in Patch #1, released on August 25, 2023.
                • NEW! - At level 3 the Bard can now freely choose three additional skill proficiencies they wish to take as of Patch #1, released on August 25, 2023, replacing the previous system where the bard always got proficiency in Arcana, Intimidation and Sleight of Hand.

                            In DnD 5e, i took College of Eloquence for my bard precisely because he can lower enemy save with BONUS action using inspirations. But i thought College of Lore was close second because it uses only REACTION to increase ally's armor class, a) allowing to use bonus action for other things, b) never wasting inspiration on allies that didn't have a chance to use them, and it happened very often (inspiration lasted only ten minutes in 5e), and c) it's extremely effective on tanks with already high armor class, allowing to close that small gap in their armor class, making them almost invincible.
                            Let's put Baldur's Gate 3 College of Lore into perspective, shall we?
                            Level 6 bard College of Lore can:
                            1) 16/day lower enemies' saves by 1d8 (4.5 average). This is better or equivalent to disadvantage that Hightened Spell gives, lvl 6 sorcerer can use it 2-5/day, bard 16/day. Already ridiculous.
                            As if that wasn't enough, unlike Hightened Spell, Cutting Words can be used AFTER casting your spell to decide if you want to spend it or not.
                            And unlike 5e College of Eloquence, it's not a bonus action, but a reaction, allowing to use bonus action for attacking, casting, or applying poison.
                            Oh, of course it can also be used to raise my or ally's armor class by 1d8, give an ally 1d8 to any attack or save without any action (before battle).
                            2) Can select 2 spells from any class, in addition to bard-exclusive spells.
                            3) Doubles the bonus of two proficiencies.
                            4) Three bonus proficiencies.
                            5) Gains half of the proficiency bonus on all other skills.
                            6) +1 bonus to initiative, dispel magic, or counterspell check. (initiative is extremely important for casters)
                            7) 5 HP/level (same as Draconic sorcerer actually)
                            8) Light armor proficiency.
                            9) Additional short rest, healing 50% of allies' hit points and restoring their short rest abilities.
                            10) Countercharm (extremely situational, but still)

                            Draconic Bloodline sorcerer has:
                            1) Better spell selection.
                            2) 4 more damage to Fireball.
                            3) 50% Resistance to your own Fireball.
                            4) 2-5/day metamagic (the best ones are Hightened Spell, Twinned Spell).
